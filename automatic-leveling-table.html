Senior Design: Automatic Underwater Sample Leveling Table for Nondestructive Evaluation

Overview

This senior design project focused on developing an automated system to precisely level samples for ultrasonic nondestructive evaluation (NDE). In ultrasonic testing, the sample surface must be perfectly perpendicular to the transducer to accurately detect internal defects. The current manual process is time-consuming, often taking lab technicians up to an hour to achieve the required precision. Our solution dramatically reduces this time to under one minute while improving accuracy to within 2-11 microns—well below the 15-micron requirement.

Technical Specifications

  • Leveling Accuracy: 2-11 microns (requirement: 15 microns)
  • Leveling Time: Under 1 minute (from 30-60 minutes manually)
  • Load Capacity: Up to 15 pounds
  • Platform Size: Triangular with 22.5-inch sides
  • Power Input: Standard 120 VAC, 60 Hz wall power
  • Motor Specification: IP68-rated waterproof stepper motors
  • Control System: Arduino-based with Python GUI interface

The Challenge

Ultrasonic nondestructive evaluation is a critical technique used across aerospace, automotive, military, and academic research to inspect manufactured parts without damaging them. The method can detect internal flaws by measuring ultrasonic wave reflections, but requires the sample surface to be precisely perpendicular to the transducer. The existing manual leveling process was extremely tedious, requiring multiple iterations of micro-adjustments using manual screws, with each attempt taking 30-60 minutes of valuable lab time.

The image above shows an additively manufactured block of steel with intentional defects inside forming the Purdue P. These defects are invisible to the naked eye but detectable with ultrasonic scanning when the sample is properly leveled.

Design Approach

Our team designed an innovative solution using stepper motors with lead screws to automatically adjust a triangular mounting platform. The system works by:

  1. First performing an initial ultrasonic scan to measure the surface orientation
  2. Calculating the required adjustments via surface height differences
  3. Precisely moving two motorized corners of the triangular platform while keeping one fixed
  4. Confirming level position with validation scans showing micron-level precision

Project Development Process

1
Problem Definition & Requirements Analysis

Worked with the lab to understand the limitations of manual leveling and defined technical requirements including 15-micron tolerance, underwater operation, and 5-minute time limit.

2
Concept Development & Selection

Evaluated multiple solutions before selecting stepper motors with lead screws due to their precision, reliability, and compatibility with underwater operation.

3
Detailed Design & Prototyping

Developed CAD models, performed mechanical calculations for load capacity and deflection, created electronic schematics, and built a preliminary cardboard prototype to validate the concept.

4
Software Development

Created a comprehensive Python-based GUI for surface analysis and motor control, implementing a region of interest feature to level a specific portion of the samples.

5
Manufacturing & Assembly

Manufactured components using waterjet cutting and precision machining, assembled electronic components with safety features, and integrated the system with the existing ultrasonic scanner.

6
Testing & Validation

Conducted rigorous testing with multiple samples, validating performance against all requirements and demonstrating exceptional improvements in both speed and accuracy.

Technical Innovations

The project incorporated several key technical innovations:

  • Precise Motion Control: Our system achieves 6.35 micrometer resolution per step using a carefully selected combination of lead screws and microstepping motor drivers.
  • Safety-Critical Design: We implemented redundant safety systems including dual 10-amp relays and an emergency stop button to prevent electrical hazards when operating in water.
  • Intelligent Software: Our custom Python GUI processes ultrasonic scan data to determine optimal adjustments.
  • Mechanical Integration: The triangular platform design with aluminum construction provides excellent stability while maintaining compatibility with the existing equipment.
  • Waterproof Operation: The final design includes IP68-rated motors capable of continuous underwater operation.

Key Challenges & Solutions

Throughout the project, we encountered and overcame several significant challenges:

  • Supply Chain Issues: When waterproof motors were delayed, we implemented a temporary solution using standard motors for validation while designing for easy upgrade to waterproof components.
  • Electrical Safety: Operating electronics underwater presented serious safety concerns, which we addressed with redundant safety systems, waterproof connectors, and comprehensive testing protocols.
  • Precision Requirements: Achieving 15-micron accuracy required extensive calibration and algorithmic refinement, ultimately exceeding requirements with 2-11 micron precision.
  • System Integration: Connecting our system to the existing ultrasonic scanner required careful coordination and custom interface development.

Market Analysis

Research indicated significant potential applications for our automatic leveling system. The US Naval research lab alone has at least 7 ultrasonic testing machines similar to the one at Purdue. Combined with Air Force and Army facilities, there could be demand for 21+ units just in military research settings. In industries where safety is critical, NDE can provide a way to reduce waste from creating redundant parts for testing. Additionally the automotive powertrain testing market is projected to grow from $11.9 billion (2020) to $19.7 billion (2025), while aerospace testing is expected to reach $18.6 billion by 2025.

Conclusion

The Automatic Underwater Sample Leveling Table project successfully delivered a solution that dramatically improves both efficiency and accuracy in ultrasonic nondestructive evaluation. By reducing leveling time from 30-60 minutes to under one minute while exceeding precision requirements, our system provides significant value to research laboratories and testing facilities. The project demonstrates how thoughtful engineering design and integration of mechanical, electronic, and software components can solve complex technical challenges and create meaningful improvements in scientific processes.
