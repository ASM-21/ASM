Foot Pedal Rake: Developing a Device to Easily Remove Leaves from a Rake

Overview

The objective of this project was to design and develop a foot-pedal rake that simplifies the process of removing leaves stuck on rake tines. The final design includes a foot pedal mechanism with a brush that slides along the tines to remove leaves, constant-force springs for smooth operation, and an adjustable handle. The product meets key engineering requirements, including a cost of less than $30, a weight of less than 4 pounds, and durability against corrosion.

Key Features

  • Foot pedal mechanism to remove leaves from rake tines.
  • Constant-force springs for smooth operation and automatic retraction.
  • Adjustable handle for user convenience.
  • Lightweight and durable construction using non-corrosive materials.

Problem Definition

The problem addressed by this project is the inconvenience of removing leaves stuck on rake tines, which makes raking leaves tedious and time-consuming. Through customer research, it was found that 75.5% of rake users remove leaves multiple times during raking, and over 40% find this task extremely annoying. The target market is homeowners who want a more efficient and enjoyable raking experience.

Primary Mechanism

The primary concept selected was a rake with a foot pedal mechanism. The foot pedal activates a brush that slides along the tines to remove leaves, while constant-force springs ensure the brush returns to its original position. The design also includes an adjustable handle and a non-corrosive, lightweight construction.

Conclusions

The foot-pedal rake design successfully addresses the problem of leaves getting stuck on rake tines. The product meets all engineering requirements and is ready for production. Future improvements include optimizing the brush bristle density and exploring injection molding for certain components.
