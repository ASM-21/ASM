Normal Shock Relations

Normal Shock Relations

Ma2^2 = (Ma1^2 + 2/(gamma-1)) / (2*gamma/(gamma-1)*Ma1^2 - 1)
P2/P1 = (2*gamma*Ma1^2 - (gamma-1)) / (gamma+1)  |  T2/T1 = P2/P1 * rho1/rho2
P02/P01 = (P2/P1) * ((T1/T2)^(gamma/(gamma-1)))  -- stagnation pressure loss across shock

Valid only for Ma1 > 1 (supersonic upstream). Shock always produces subsonic downstream flow (Ma2 < 1) for a normal shock.

Ma1 must be greater than 1.

Dimensionless Number Reference & Quick-Calc

Reynolds Number (Re)

Re = rho*v*L / mu = v*L / nu   |   Inertial forces / Viscous forces

Physical meaning: Ratio of inertial to viscous forces. Governs laminar-turbulent transition and dynamic similarity for viscous flows.

Typical ranges: Pipe flow laminar Re < 2300; turbulent Re > 4000. Flat plate transition Re ~ 5e5. Creeping flow Re < 1.

Froude Number (Fr)

Fr = v / sqrt(g*L)   |   Inertial forces / Gravitational forces

Physical meaning: Ratio of flow velocity to wave propagation speed. Governs free-surface and open-channel flows. Fr = 1 is the critical (choked) condition.

Fr < 1 subcritical; Fr = 1 critical; Fr > 1 supercritical. Ships: Fr ~ 0.1–0.5. Spillways: Fr ~ 0.1–10.

Weber Number (We)

We = rho*v^2*L / sigma   |   Inertial forces / Surface tension forces

Physical meaning: Ratio of inertial to surface tension forces. Governs droplet breakup, bubble formation, thin films, and two-phase flows. High We means surface tension is negligible.

We < 1: surface tension dominant. We ~ 1–10: transition. We > 12: droplet breakup. Atomisers: We ~ 10^2–10^4.

Mach Number (Ma)

Ma = v / a   |   a = sqrt(gamma*R*T)   |   Inertial / Compressibility forces

Physical meaning: Ratio of flow velocity to local speed of sound. Governs compressibility effects. Ma < 0.3 is effectively incompressible.

Subsonic Ma < 1; Transonic 0.8–1.2; Supersonic 1–5; Hypersonic Ma > 5.

Ma

Euler Number (Eu)

Eu = DeltaP / (rho*v^2)   |   Pressure forces / Inertial forces

Physical meaning: Ratio of pressure difference to dynamic pressure. Used in cavitation analysis (cavitation number Ca = Eu using vapour pressure), pressure drop correlations, and pump/fan performance.

Eu = 1 means pressure drop equals dynamic head. Cavitation occurs when Eu (with Pv) approaches zero from above.

Eu
Euler number--

Strouhal Number (St)

St = f*L / v   |   Unsteady / Convective inertia

Physical meaning: Ratio of oscillation frequency to convective timescale. Governs vortex shedding, oscillating flows, and acoustic resonance in bluff body wakes.

Karman vortex shedding: St ~ 0.2 for cylinders (Re = 100–1e5). St >> 1 means oscillation is fast relative to convection.

St
Strouhal number--

Prandtl Number (Pr)

Pr = mu*cp / k = nu / alpha   |   Momentum diffusivity / Thermal diffusivity

Physical meaning: Ratio of viscous to thermal diffusion. Pr < 1 means heat diffuses faster than momentum (liquid metals). Pr > 1 means momentum diffuses faster (oils).

Liquid metals: 0.001–0.03. Air: ~0.71. Water: 1.7–13.6. Engine oil: 50–100,000. Mercury: ~0.025.

Pr
Prandtl number--

Nusselt Number (Nu)

Nu = h*L / k   |   Convective heat transfer / Conductive heat transfer

Physical meaning: Enhanced heat transfer due to convection relative to pure conduction. Nu = 1 means conduction only. Nu >> 1 means convection dominates.

Forced convection (pipes): Nu ~ 10–1000. Natural convection: Nu ~ 1–100. Boiling/condensation: Nu ~ 1000–100,000.

Nu
Nusselt number--

Biot Number (Bi)

Bi = h*L / k_solid   |   Surface convection / Internal conduction (solid)

Physical meaning: Ratio of convective heat transfer at the surface to conductive heat transfer inside a solid. Bi < 0.1 means the lumped capacitance assumption is valid (uniform temperature inside solid).

Bi < 0.1: lumped capacitance valid. Bi ~ 1: transition. Bi >> 1: surface resistance negligible vs. internal resistance.

Capillary Number (Ca)

Ca = mu*v / sigma   |   Viscous forces / Surface tension forces

Physical meaning: Ratio of viscous to surface tension forces. Governs flow in capillaries, microfluidics, enhanced oil recovery, and coating flows. Ca = We/Re.

Ca < 1e-5: capillary-dominated. Ca ~ 1: viscous and surface tension comparable. Ca > 1: viscous flow dominates, interface deforms.
