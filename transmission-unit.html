Transmission Design: Machine Design Project

Project Overview

This project involved designing a transmission system for a powersports vehicle, including input and output shafts, bearings, gears, and keys. The design was based on fatigue loading, stress analysis, and failure criteria to ensure reliability and performance under specified operating conditions.

System Architecture

The transmission system consists of:

  • Input Shaft: Designed for fatigue loading using Von-Mises stress approximations and Goodman failure criteria.
  • Output Shaft: Similar design approach as the input shaft, with considerations for stress concentration points.
  • Bearings: Selected based on radial and axial loads, with a design life of 15,000 hours.
  • Gears: Designed for a 4:1 gear ratio, with Grade 3 Nitrided Steel as the material.
  • Keys: Designed to prevent failure by shear and crushing, with a factor of safety of 1.5.

Input Shaft Design

The input shaft was designed to handle repeated loads from the motor and pinion. Key design parameters include:

  • Material: AISI 1020 CD Steel
  • Diameter: 1.35 in. (small), 1.5 in. (large)
  • Retaining Ring: McMaster-Carr SKU 6420N119
Stress concentration points were analyzed using Goodman criteria, and the design was validated for fatigue and yield failure.

Output Shaft Design

The output shaft was designed to handle loads from the gear. Key design parameters include:

  • Material: AISI 1020 CD Steel
  • Diameter: 1.772 in. (small), 2 in. (large)
  • Retaining Ring: McMaster-Carr External Retaining Ring for 1-13/16" OD
The design was validated for fatigue and yield failure at stress concentration points.

Bearing Selection

Bearings were selected based on radial and axial loads, with a design life of 15,000 hours and 90% reliability. The following bearings are recommended:

  • Input Shaft: SKF 207 and SKF 6407
  • Output Shaft: SKF 16009 and SKF 209

Gear Design

The pinion and spur gear were designed for a 4:1 gear ratio. Key design parameters include:

  • Pinion: 16 teeth, 3.2 in. pitch diameter, Grade 3 Nitrided Steel
  • Spur Gear: 64 teeth, 12.8 in. pitch diameter, Grade 3 Nitrided Steel
Contact stresses and factors of safety for wear and bending were calculated to validate the design.

Key Design

Keys were designed to prevent failure by shear and crushing. Key parameters include:

  • Pinion Key: 1.5 in. length, 0.1 in. height and thickness
  • Gear Key: 1.75 in. length, 0.1 in. height and thickness

Conclusion

The transmission design successfully meets all specified requirements, including fatigue life, stress limits, and power transmission. Key takeaways include:

  • The importance of stress concentration analysis in shaft design.
  • The role of material selection in ensuring reliability and performance.
  • The need for precise calculations in gear and bearing selection.
This project provided valuable experience in machine design and failure analysis, which will be applicable in future engineering projects.
