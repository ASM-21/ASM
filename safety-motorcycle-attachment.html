S.E.A.T. — Safe, Ergonomic, Assistive Transport Module

Project Video

Overview

The S.E.A.T. (Safe, Ergonomic, Assistive Transport Module) addresses a critical need in the transportation landscape of India and similar developing markets where two-wheelers serve as the primary mode of family transportation. Families using two-wheelers struggle to safely transport children, often resorting to makeshift solutions that significantly increase accident risks. Our solution is a secure, affordable seating system specifically designed for children on two-wheelers that features integrated safety components and ergonomic design.

Market Opportunity

  • Over 221 million registered two-wheelers in India
  • Annual sales exceeding 15 million units
  • Primary transportation method for entire families, including young children
  • Growing safety awareness among Indian parents in urban and semi-urban areas

Key Features

  • Fixed-height seat with integrated side arms and backrest for stability and support
  • Reliable safety belt with intuitive click-to-lock mechanism
  • Constructed from lightweight yet durable materials (LDPE/rotomolded plastic with foam inserts)
  • Ergonomically designed to support the lumbar curve from L1 to L5 region
  • Support for the slight upper thoracic curve from T12 to T9
  • Universal mounting system compatible with popular scooter models

Ergonomic Design

A detailed ergonomic study was conducted to ensure optimal comfort and safety for users. The design incorporates anthropometric data from established standards including SAE J826, ISO 7250, and University of Michigan Transportation Research Institute studies. The seat provides support for the natural lumbar curve and slight upper thoracic curve, enhancing comfort for individuals of various body types, including children and those with physical disabilities.

Prototype Development

Our physical prototype was constructed using readily available materials including foam board, cardboard, and duct tape. This low-fidelity approach allowed us to quickly materialize our design concepts while maintaining flexibility for iterative modifications. Through interaction with the prototype, we gained valuable insights into the dimensional requirements of our design and received user feedback that informed subsequent refinements.

Business Model

The S.E.A.T. is strategically positioned at the intersection of child safety equipment and two-wheeler accessories—categories that have rarely been effectively combined in the Indian market. With a target price point of ₹2000-3000 (~$25-40), the product is affordable for middle-class Indian families while ensuring sustainable margins. Our go-to-market strategy emphasizes education about child safety on two-wheelers alongside product promotion, with initial market entry focused on high-density urban areas with significant two-wheeler usage.

Conclusions

The S.E.A.T. project demonstrates successful application of mechanical engineering principles to address a significant safety challenge in transportation. Through methodical design iteration and market analysis, we created a safety system for children on two-wheelers that maintains safety standards while achieving an affordable price point. The modular design not only facilitates repair and replacement but also supports extended product lifecycles through secondary markets, aligning with sustainable engineering practices.
